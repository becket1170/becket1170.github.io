Current project

I am a medievalist working at the intersection of hagiography, manuscript studies, and rhetorical theory. My doctoral dissertation — completed at the University of Saskatchewan under the supervision of Dr. Yin Liu — examined the Thomas Becket legend in the South English Legendary, attending to questions of genre, material transmission, and the construction of lay readership in the thirteenth and fourteenth centuries.

My current book project develops from the dissertation into a broader study of archiepiscopal sanctity: the SEL lives of Becket, Dunstan, and Alphege in their manuscript contexts, with attention to the rhetorical shaping of ecclesiastical authority for vernacular audiences.

Article in progress

I am at work on "The Outspoken Rhetoric and the Constitutions of Clarendon," which reads the SEL Becket poet's rhetorical reordering of the constitutional articles through Booth's Rhetoric of Fiction and Rabinowitz's audience theory. The article is under preparation for submission to Rhetor.

Long-term

A collaborative project under development will produce a critical edition of the SEL archiepiscopal saints, incorporating digital manuscript transcription and codicological analysis. I am particularly interested in the methodological questions raised by computational approaches to Middle English manuscript witnesses — questions of paleographic encoding, witness collation, and the limits of automation in the reading of damaged or ambiguous hands.
